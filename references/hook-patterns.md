# Hook Patterns

The hook is the variable that matters most. Same creator, same offer, different first line = 2-3x CAC swing. This is the library.

## How to use this doc

For any campaign, pick 3-4 patterns from the table below. Generate 2-3 variations of each. Test all of them. The winners earn more spend; the rest get cut by day 7.

Don't pick all "problem statement" hooks. Spread across patterns so you learn which pattern matches your audience, not just which hook in one pattern.

## The patterns

### 1. Problem statement

> "Stop [bad thing your customer does]."  
> "[Pain point] is killing your [outcome]."  
> "If you're still [bad practice], read this."

**When it works:** the pain is named and obvious. The viewer self-identifies in the first 2 seconds.

**When it fails:** if the "problem" is something the viewer doesn't actually believe is a problem.

**Examples (B2B SaaS):**
- "Stop running Google Ads without profit data joined to your campaigns."
- "If you're using AI to write your LinkedIn posts, you're publishing slop."

### 2. Contrarian belief

> "Everyone says [X]. They're wrong."  
> "[Industry orthodoxy] is the worst advice in [category]."  
> "Stop doing [popular tactic]. Here's what works."

**When it works:** you have a sharp, defensible POV. Contrarian without proof reads as edgy bait.

**When it fails:** if you can't back the contrarian claim within the next 10 seconds.

**Examples:**
- "ROAS is a lie. Here's the metric that actually tells you if your ads are profitable."
- "Every newsletter guide tells you to write daily. The best newsletters publish weekly."

### 3. Specific result

> "How [named person] hit [number] in [timeframe]."  
> "[Real number] in [real timeframe] using [your product]."  
> "[Customer] went from [before] to [after] in [period]."

**When it works:** you have a real customer story with a real number.

**When it fails:** if the number is round and unverifiable ("3x'd their revenue"). Specificity is everything.

**Examples:**
- "How Sarah at [company] cut her CAC 41% in 60 days by changing one bidding strategy."
- "From $4 CPM to $1.20 in 11 days using audience-list lookalikes seeded from buyers, not all-customers."

### 4. Curiosity gap

> "I tried [X] for 30 days. Here's what happened."  
> "What I learned after [number] [unit] of [activity]."  
> "The [adjective] truth about [topic]."

**When it works:** your category is novel. Viewer doesn't know what to expect.

**When it fails:** if the payoff doesn't deliver. Clickbait without substance burns trust fast.

**Examples:**
- "I ran a $10,000 test on Reddit ads for 30 days. Here's what worked and what didn't."
- "We turned off our brand search campaign for 14 days. Revenue went up. Here's why."

### 5. Direct offer

> "Get [product] for [price]. [Urgency or deadline]."  
> "[Discount %] off [product] until [date]."  
> "[Specific benefit] for [specific price]."

**When it works:** price is a real edge, the offer is genuinely scarce, your brand is already known.

**When it fails:** for unknown brands trying to compete on price.

**Examples:**
- "Lifetime access to [product] for $79. 3 days left."
- "The full [course name] for $49 instead of $499. This week only."

### 6. Question

> "What do [ICP] do when [bad situation]?"  
> "How are you handling [emerging trend]?"  
> "Are you doing [common bad thing]?"

**When it works:** the question makes the ICP self-select. People who don't relate scroll past, which is fine.

**When it fails:** if the question is generic ("Want more customers?").

**Examples:**
- "What do solo founders do when their email list stops growing?"
- "Are you still using GA4's default attribution model? Read this first."

### 7. Pattern interrupt

> "STOP scrolling. This is for [specific ICP] only."  
> "I'm only saying this once."  
> "[Bold visual cue] [unexpected line]."

**When it works:** high-volume cold audiences where the goal is to break the scroll pattern. Reels, TikTok, IG cold prospecting.

**When it fails:** for warm audiences who already know you. Reads as desperate.

**Examples:**
- "STOP scrolling if you run paid ads. This is the data Google's reps don't want you to see."
- "Quick question for performance marketers only."

### 8. Personal admission

> "I used to think [thing]. Then [event]. Now I [new behavior]."  
> "Here's something embarrassing I learned recently."  
> "I was wrong about [topic]. Here's what changed my mind."

**When it works:** for founder-led brands. Audiences want to know the human, and admission signals trustworthiness.

**When it fails:** if the admission is performative ("I used to be perfect at this. Now I'm even better.")

**Examples:**
- "I used to think LinkedIn ads were a waste. Then I ran a $30K test."
- "We launched on Product Hunt and got 4 customers. Here's what I'd do differently."

### 9. Demonstration

> "Watch what happens when [action]."  
> "Here's [process] in real-time."  
> "[Tangible thing] in [short timeframe]."

**When it works:** for visually-driven products where the demonstration IS the value. UGC heavy.

**When it fails:** for abstract or service businesses where there's nothing to "show."

**Examples:**
- "Watch what happens when you run this Google Ads audit script."
- "Here's me writing a LinkedIn post with [tool] in real time."

### 10. List

> "[N] [things] that [outcome]."  
> "[N] reasons your [thing] isn't [outcome]."  
> "[N] [people / brands / tools] doing [thing] differently."

**When it works:** for content-heavy posts where the list IS the value. Carousel ads, long-form static.

**When it fails:** if N is round and the items are obvious. "5 ways to grow your business" is dead on arrival.

**Examples:**
- "7 paid-ads metrics Google reps don't want you to obsess over."
- "12 newsletter operators who write less than 500 words and still get 30%+ open rates."

## How to test across patterns

Run 4 hooks per ad set. One from each of 4 different patterns. After 7 days:

- The hook with the highest CTR + lowest CPA wins
- The pattern that worked tells you something about your audience
- The pattern that didn't tells you which mode of communication doesn't land

Then for the next round, generate 4 more from the winning pattern + 2 from a new pattern. Repeat. Six rounds in, you have a sharp sense of what hooks your ICP responds to.

## What never works

- Anything starting with "In today's fast-paced..." or "In the ever-evolving..."
- "Are you ready to unlock..."
- "Imagine if you could..."
- Anything that promises generic transformation without naming a specific outcome
- Hooks built around hashtags
- Hooks that use emojis as substitute for substance

Cut these on sight.
