# UGC Script Template

A 30-60 second script the creator can follow. Bracket-formatted so timing, action, and dialogue are all explicit.

## The structure

```
[0:00 - HOOK]
[Action / setting]
"Spoken line."

[0:03 - PIVOT]
[Action]
"Spoken line."

[0:08 - DEMO]
[Action - show the product]
"Spoken line."

[0:18 - PROOF]
[Action]
"Spoken line with one real number or outcome."

[0:30 - CTA]
[Action]
"Spoken line with offer + how to act."
```

## Section-by-section rules

### Hook (0:00 - 0:03)

- First 3 seconds determine whether anyone keeps watching
- Direct-to-camera, no preamble
- Stop-the-scroll energy
- Pick a pattern from `hook-patterns.md` and write the line accordingly
- Never start with "Hey guys" or "What's up everyone"

### Pivot (0:03 - 0:08)

- Acknowledge the viewer's problem in one line
- Connect the hook to the rest of the script
- Earn the next 25 seconds

### Demo (0:08 - 0:18)

- Show the product. Specific feature, not a vague tour.
- The creator's hands on the product, screen recording, or a real workflow shot
- Voiceover names what's happening: "This is where I [specific action] and [specific result]"
- No b-roll for b-roll's sake

### Proof (0:18 - 0:30)

- One real number, one real outcome
- Named customer if possible
- The viewer needs evidence to believe the demo's promise
- "Last week I [outcome]" or "[Customer] hit [number] in [timeframe]"

### CTA (0:30 - 0:45 or shorter)

- The offer, the URL, the code
- One single action
- No more than 12 seconds total — viewer attention drops fast
- "Get [product] at [URL]. Code [code] for [discount]." Done.

## A working example (B2B SaaS)

```
[0:00 - HOOK]
[Creator at desk, looking directly at camera, slight irritation]
"If you're a paid marketer and you're still using Google's reported ROAS to make budget decisions, you're losing money. Let me show you."

[0:03 - PIVOT]
[Quick cut to Google Ads dashboard, ROAS prominently displayed]
"This says 5.2x ROAS. Looks great. But it's a lie. Here's why."

[0:08 - DEMO]
[Cut to actual data — Looker / Sheet — comparing platform ROAS to real profit]
"When I joined this campaign data to my actual order data, the real profit-to-spend was 1.1x. Not 5.2x. The platform was taking credit for conversions that would have happened from email and direct anyway."

[0:18 - PROOF]
[Cut to closeup on a specific number — "$2,847 → $310 profit"]
"On a $2,847 spend last month, real profit was $310. Google reported $14,800 in 'conversion value.' This is the gap that's bankrupting performance marketers."

[0:30 - CTA]
[Back to creator, direct to camera]
"Get the free guide at nickbuilds.ai/ppc. Link in bio. It's 8 pages and it'll save you thousands."
```

## What separates a winning UGC script from an okay one

| Okay | Winning |
|---|---|
| Generic hook | Specific to the audience's exact frustration |
| Generic demo | A specific feature with a specific outcome |
| Made-up proof number | A real number from a real customer (or your own data) |
| Two CTAs | One CTA |
| 60+ seconds | 30-45 seconds, tight |
| One creator does all 5 hooks | One creator per hook variation — test 3-5 creators |

## How to use this with creators

Send the creator:

1. The full script in bracket format
2. The product (a free account, a sample, a demo login)
3. Visual references — competitor ads they should NOT look like, plus 2-3 of your past winners
4. A list of hard nos: things they should NOT say or show
5. The brief in one paragraph: who this is for, what it must do

Then let them improvise. A 5% deviation from the script that sounds like them is better than a perfectly-recited script.

## When to skip UGC entirely

If your category requires technical accuracy (medical, financial, legal), pre-written-and-recited is safer. UGC's strength is authenticity, and authenticity sometimes wanders into territory you can't legally back.

For most B2B SaaS, DTC, and consumer products: UGC scripted in this format wins against polished studio ads at most price points and audience sizes.
